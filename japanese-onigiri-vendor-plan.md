# Japanese Onigiri Vendor

## Objective
Launch a compliant, differentiated, and scalable authentic Japanese onigiri vendor concept and secure placement at the Winter Garden Farmers Market (or comparable local markets), while building early proof of demand.

## Phase 1: Define & Position (Week 1)
**Goal:** Establish a clear, compelling concept aligned with market expectations

**Actions**
- Develop business concept summary (authentic, handmade Japanese rice balls + simple foods)
- Create brand positioning statement (authenticity, simplicity, cultural experience)
- Finalize focused starter menu (4–6 onigiri + 1-2 add-ons)
- Select business name + visual direction (clean, minimalist Japanese aesthetic)

**Deliverables**
- 1-page concept summary
- Menu + pricing draft
- Brand positioning statement

## Phase 2: Compliance & Setup (Weeks 1–2)
**Goal:** Ensure full legal and operational readiness

**Actions**
- Register business + obtain local license (if required)
- Determine regulatory path via FDACS or DBPR
- Secure food safety certification (ServSafe recommended)
- Identify/confirm commissary kitchen (if required)
- Obtain general liability insurance

**Deliverables**
- Licensing checklist completed
- Food safety plan (rice handling, storage, transport)
- Approved prep location (if applicable)

## Phase 3: Financial & Operational Planning (Week 2)
**Goal:** Validate economic viability and execution model

**Actions**
- Build startup budget (~$2K–$3K lean target)
- Calculate unit economics (cost per onigiri, pricing, margins)
- Define daily sales targets (break-even + profit scenarios)
- Map operations workflow (prep → transport → sales → cleanup)

**Deliverables**
- Startup budget
- Pricing + margin model
- Simple sales forecast (low / expected / strong scenarios)

## Phase 4: Brand, Product & Booth (Weeks 2–3)
**Goal:** Create a professional, differentiated presentation

**Actions**
- Develop product samples (test consistency + taste)
- Design packaging (eco-friendly, clean, grab-and-go)
- Create booth setup (tent, table, signage, layout)
- Capture high-quality product + booth photos

**Deliverables**
- Finalized menu + product specs
- Packaging solution
- Booth design concept
- Application-ready photos

## Phase 5: Market Entry Strategy (Weeks 3–4)
**Goal:** Secure placement and/or build proof of concept

**Actions**
- Submit application to Winter Garden Farmers Market
- Identify 2–3 alternative local markets or pop-ups
- Launch pilot sales to validate demand
- Collect customer feedback (taste, price, presentation)

**Deliverables**
- Completed vendor application
- List of target markets
- Customer feedback summary
- Sales performance data

## Phase 6: Scale & Optimize (Ongoing)
**Goal:** Strengthen acceptance odds and grow revenue

**Actions**
- Refine menu based on sales data
- Improve speed, consistency, and presentation
- Reapply (if needed) with proof of traction
- Explore expansion (catering, events, additional markets)

**Deliverables**
- Updated menu + pricing
- Documented sales metrics
- Enhanced application (if resubmitting)

## Critical Success Factors
- Clear authentic positioning (not fusion or novelty)
- Tight, efficient menu (speed + consistency)
- Strong visual presentation + branding
- Demonstrated understanding of food safety compliance
- Proof of demand if initial entry is delayed

## Bottom Line
This is a low-cost, high-differentiation concept with strong fit for farmers markets. Success depends on disciplined execution: clear positioning, regulatory readiness, clean presentation, and a staged market-entry strategy.
